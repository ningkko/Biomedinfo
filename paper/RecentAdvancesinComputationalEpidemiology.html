To highlight some recent advances in infectious diseases, focusing specifically on modeling, data mining, and inferential and planning questions.

Epidemics, modeling, data mining, inference, planning

  1. Modeling Epidemics: An Interaction Based Approach
  1. Rate-based differential equation models
  1. partition the population into subgroups based on various criteria (e.g., demographic characteristics and disease states)
  2. use differential equation models to describe the disease dynamics across these groups.

  1. E.g.   disease dynamics characterized by a parameter , R_0, the basic reproduction number.

  1. Data collection:   via surveys, social media, sensors and policy documents -> analyzed to yield contextual situational representations .

  1. Difficulties: impossible to obtain an accurate, detailed, time-varying urban-scale human social contact network by simple measurements.

  1. Synthetic Information Environments

  1. Structure:

  1. A   statistical model of the population of interest (synthetic population):

  1. Integrate census data with other demographic and geographic data to create a population of individual agents.
  2. Generate a detailed minute-byminute schedule for each individual in the synthetic population, using time-use surveys combined with machine learning techniques (e.g. CART).
  3. Geo-locate activities using business survey information and, using a gravity model, each individual is associated with particular activity locations.

  1. A n activity based model of the social contact network

  • Created based on the data.
  • Sometimes referred to as generative social science

  1. models of disease progression

  1. Each individual is endowed with a within-host disease model represented using probabilistic timed transition systems (PTTS) .
  2. Individual level demographic variations (immunity, age, etc.) can be incorporated within the framework.
  3. Individual PTTS are coupled via the social contact network described earlier.
  4. High performance computer simulations are used to understand the spread of the contagion over the network of PTTS.

  1. models for representing and evaluating interventions, public policies and individual behavioral adaptations

  1.   E.g. Difference between real life conductivity and what’ s predicted. E.g. Prevention of the super-spreader.
  2. But data mining and machine learning could be used to identify surrogates (i.e., combinations of demographic and social attributes) that can redescribe the super-spreader property.
